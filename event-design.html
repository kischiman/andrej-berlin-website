The problem no one names

The industry calls it founder failure. It isn't.

Most builders aren't short on talent. They have a sharp vision for a better world, a great team, and a huge market. But the insatiable ambition bites back — burnout, obsession over AI implementation, deep technical rabbit holes. The product stays unfinished; the future, uncertain.

Meanwhile the clock runs: runways shorten, skills fade, and authoritarian technologies quietly narrow our freedom to build alternatives.

It's not a character flaw. It's biology, neurology, and environment at once — and all three can be addressed, fast.

What I do

I design the environment. The founders create the magic.

I create spaces that facilitate creativity and human connection, give people a direction toward work that carries their meaning, and use technology to compress the distance between idea and shipped experiment. What comes out are decentralized systems that support a network-stage economy — built by people who stay sovereign in the process.

Three formats.

01 / Workshops

Up to 90 minutes

Online or offline

A focused, high-energy session that builds collaborative momentum toward new projects, new spaces, and new experiments. Concrete actionable steps when the session ends.

02 / One-day experiences

One day

In person

A designed social experience — a meaningful day structured for real connection rather than networking, and remembered long after it ends.

03 / Two-week residencies

Two weeks

Residential · Small cohort

A small cohort of entrepreneurs, founders, and their teams at a similar stage, working the full protocol. Body, mind, product. Residents leave unblocked and shipping, with better health, real momentum, and a private network of peers who get it. Every resident is tested before and after: biomarkers, cognitive performance, and output on the thing they're building.
