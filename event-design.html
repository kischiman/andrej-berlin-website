The problem no one names

The industry calls it founder failure. It isn't.

Most builders aren't short on talent. They have a sharp vision for a better world, a great team, and a huge market. But the insatiable ambition bites back — burnout, obsession over AI implementation, deep technical rabbit holes. The product stays unfinished; the future, uncertain.

Meanwhile the clock runs: runways shorten, skills fade, and authoritarian technologies quietly narrow our freedom to build alternatives.

It's not a character flaw. It's biology, neurology, and environment at once — and all three can be addressed, fast.

What I do

I design the environment. The people do the magic.

I create spaces that facilitate creativity and human connection, give people a direction toward work that carries their meaning, and use technology to compress the distance between idea and shipped experiment. What comes out are decentralized systems that support a network-stage economy — built by people who stay sovereign in the process.

How it works

Three formats. One logic.

The same approach at three depths — from a single spark to a full reset. Each format stands alone, and each one feeds the next.

01 / Workshop

Up to 90 minutes

Online or offline

A focused, high-energy session that builds collaborative momentum toward new projects, new spaces, and new experiments — concrete enough to launch when the session ends.

02 / One-day experience

One day

In person

A designed social experience — a meaningful day structured for real connection rather than networking, and remembered long after it ends.

03 / Two-week residency

Two weeks

Residential · Small cohort

A small cohort of founders at a similar stage, working the full protocol — body, mind, product. Residents leave unblocked and shipping, with better health, real momentum, and a private network of entrepreneurs who get it. Every resident is tested before and after: biomarkers, cognitive performance, and output on the thing they're building.

Longevity & biotech

Biology is part of the build.

I blend experience design with my work in data, longevity, and biotech. In residency settings, participants who choose to can work with compounds and protocols — peptides as a performance accelerator, guided psychedelic work, gene therapies — to make the most of their time and themselves.

My role is facilitation: experimentation, research, knowledge sharing, and network weaving among those who want to explore these technologies.

01

02

Research

03

04

Connecting the people experimenting with these technologies to each other — across residencies, zones, and disciplines.
